# Research: Student Behavior - Classes Shifting Online

## Context
In the Philippines, "flexible learning" often means classes can shift from face-to-face to online with little notice due to various factors (weather, faculty availability, university-wide events, or sudden room reassignments).

## Observed Behaviors
1.  **The "Roaming" Phase**: When a class is cancelled or moved online, students who are already on campus immediately begin "roaming" for a place to sit. This is inefficient and stressful.
2.  **Group Coordination**: Students rarely study alone. The first instinct is to message the group chat (Messenger/Discord) to coordinate a meeting spot.
3.  **Connectivity & Power Needs**: Priority is given to spots with strong university Wi-Fi or data reception, and crucially, proximity to power outlets (charging ports are a premium).
4.  **Campus vs. Off-Campus**: If campus spots (libraries, open halls) are full, students spill out into nearby "study cafes" or fast-food chains (Jollibee, McDonald's, etc.) that offer a "tambayan" (hanging out) friendly environment.
5.  **Noise Tolerance**: Students have varying noise tolerances. Some prefer the silence of a library, while others thrive in the "white noise" of a busy canteen.
6.  **"Spot Saving"**: It is common for one student to arrive early and "save" spots for their friends, often leading to conflicts if the spot is perceived as "public."

## Primary Constraints
- **Battery Life**: The need for outlets is the single biggest factor for long sessions.
- **Data Cost**: High cost of mobile data makes free Wi-Fi spots highly desirable.
- **Physical Comfort**: Ventilation (electric fans or AC) is critical in the Philippine climate.
- **Safety**: Students prefer spots that are well-lit and have some level of security for their belongings.
